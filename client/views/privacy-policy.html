Helper's Privacy Policy

We at Helper take your privacy seriously and want to be open and transparent about how your data is handled and used. We provide the following Privacy Policy explaining the ways in which we collect and use any personal information.

Collection of Routine Information

Helper tracks basic information about the vists and requests to the underlying application servers. This information includes, but is not limited to, IP addresses, browser details such as User Agent, tiemstamps, and referring pages. None of this information can be used in any way to identify specific visitors to the system and is only used for routine server administration and maintenance purposes.

In layman's terms: We log some data about your requests and will look at those logs when we have a bug and need to understand what caused the issue and how to fix it. If things go well, we will never even look at those logs and that information. Ever.

Cookies

We use Cookies for authentication when you sign into Helper. Beyond that, we do not use any Cookies, tracking scripts, or Third-Party Services. We don't like that model of operating and don't want to clog up your bandwidth with tracking beacons.

User Profile Information

When you sign up to be a Helper you use an email and when you fill out your profile, you may enter your address, phone number, and other identifying information. We do not look at this information, we do not sell that information, and we delete all instances of stale data when you update or delete your profile. As such, all deletes or updates are one-way operations and cannot be undone.

If you have signed up and want to remove your information from our system, you can delete your account and all information will be purged within 24 hours. Your comments, media, and other data that was created by you will be deleted and purged from our records but any replies will be kept, simply referring a Deleted Item. We reserve the right to store non Identifying Information such as timestamps and Universal Unique Identifiers for admnistrative and retrosepctive purposes.

Your password is encrypted using a military-grade algorithm used by OpenBSD and other Linux distributions for password hashing. We do not log your password, plaintext or otherwise, ever and no administrative user access your password for any reason. If you forget your password, you can request a reset but we cannot tell you what it is because we honestly don't know!
